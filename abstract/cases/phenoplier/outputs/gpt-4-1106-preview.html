[PASS] (1.00) While transcriptome-wide association studies have advanced our understanding of individual gene roles in disease mechanisms, the complex architecture of traits suggests that gene-gene interactions are pivotal in disease etiology and progression. This gap in knowledge underscores the need for a comprehensive approach to elucidate the interplay between gene expression and complex traits, particularly for the development of novel therapies. Here we present PhenoPLIER, a novel computational method that integrates gene-trait associations with pharmacological data into a unified latent space, facilitating the analysis of gene co-expression modules under various conditions. Our findings reveal significant associations between diseases and gene modules expressed in relevant cell types. PhenoPLIER accurately predicts known drug-disease relationships and provides insights into drug mechanisms of action. Additionally, our approach successfully identifies key regulatory genes in lipid metabolism that are not directly associated with traits but are highlighted within trait-associated modules, as demonstrated by a CRISPR screen.
